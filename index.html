This short story is for children age from 4-12

Class 1 of Sunny Meadows Woodland School embarked on their first Shadow Woods adventure, but Hank, a struggling hedgehog, was left alone due to Matilda Mouse's perceived oddity.

Miss Squirrel enjoyed discussing Sunny Meadow's beautiful flowers with children, creating a rainbow-like effect, despite the delayed journey due to Mr. Mole's broken tunnel train.

Matilda Mouse, Rachel Rabbit, Wendy Weasel, and Ralph Racoon encountered a large creature in Shadow Woods, Hank, nervous and lacking friends. Matilda sparked a group of friends to join her.

Miss Squirrel was not happy about that and so she shouted for Matilda to come back. Then off she chased after her. Soon the whole class followed and before they knew it, they were right in the middle of the woods. Now instead of bright sunshine, only shadows scattered before them.

The class stopped, awestruck by the sight of Miss Squirrel leaving Matilda behind, while Rachel Rabbit, Wendy Weasel, and Ralph Racoon, along with Hank, remained silent in the woods.

Matilda mouse screamed "BOO!", causing everyone to laugh, except Hank, who Matilda called "silly Hank" and deemed him unfun.

A hungry snake surprised the class, revealing lunchtime. Miss Squirrel had left Matilda alone, leaving the class fearful. Matilda and her friends gathered in fear.

Hank saved the class by pointing to a hole under a chestnut tree. The animals jumped down, but Matilda was angry. Hank flipped himself into a ball and blocked the hole with spikes, ensuring no lunch for the snake today.

Miss Squirrel informs Mr. Owl about a snake's escape from Hank's spikes. The class thanks Hank, who Matilda hugs and apologizes for being odd. Hank, unused to attention, turns pink and bounces as his new friends cheer him up.
